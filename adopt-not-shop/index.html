Once Upon A Time

there lived two sister puppies named Penny and Mitzy. They cared for each other a lot.

They didnt understand why they were in a shelter

they wondered why their owner left them.

They spent everyday, wondering when they will find a new home.

One by one, friends disappeared

Some were fortunate enough to find a new home, but for the rest, not so much.

And the other shelter mates started to talk

"Where do you think they are?"
"Hope they're alive!"
"ARE WE NEXT???"
Mitzy & Penny became frightened.
What will happen to them if they dont find a new home? How much time do they have till then?

Each day was very sad and lonely

They confided in each other but that got old pretty fast. All they could do was to hold onto a tiny strand of hope.

Then one day...

Mitzy & Penny Found a Home!

About 2.7 million healthy, adoptable cats and dogs—about one every 11 seconds—are put down in U.S. shelters each year. Often these animals are the offspring of cherished family pets. Spay/neuter is a proven way to reduce pet overpopulation, ensuring that every pet has a family to love them.

Many cats and dogs who die as a result of pet overpopulation could have made wonderful pets.

A solution is possible and starts with each of us taking one small step: getting our own pets spayed or neutered. To help stop pet overpopulation further, consider adopting your next pet from an animal shelter.
